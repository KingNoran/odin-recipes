Description

The "Adobo" is a soysauce-vinegar-based filipino dish. It is the most popular dish of the country, and it can be cooked regularly or with a spicy kick.

Ingredients

  • 10 cloves Garlic
  • 1 pack of Soy Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Oyster Sauce
  • 1 tsp Whole Black Peppercorns
  • 2 pounds of Pork Belly
  • 1.5 pounds of Chicken (preferrably drumsticks and thigh)
  • 2 tbsp Cooking Oil
  • 1 Large Onion/Shallots
  • 3 Bay Leaves
  • 2 packs of Cane Vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • Some sugar
  • Red Chilis (optional)

Steps

  1. Slice the garlic and onions/shallots. Prepare the chilis as well if you have them.
  2. Sautee the garlic, onions, and chilis. When fragrant, add the chicken and pork. Sautee for a few minutes.
  3. Deglaze the pan by adding water. When the water boils, add the peppercorns and simmer for a few minutes.
  4. Give the Adobo a 2-to-1 ratio of vinegar and soy sauce. Add first the soy sauce, simmer for a few minutes, then add the vinegar and let it simmer again.
  5. Add the oysters sauce, bay leaves, and sugar. Simmer for one minute before serving.
